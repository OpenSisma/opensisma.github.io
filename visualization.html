Deep dive into OpenSisma

Welcome to the OpenSisma visualization tool. Click the tabs above to navigate through the graphs derived from our datasets.

If you wish to explore the map, instead, there are more ways to do that:

  • Through a choropleth layer map created accordingly to the microseismic scale Mercalli-Cancani-Sieberg;
  • Feature groups with three types of reconstruction works progress:

    • Red markers for works that have never started;
    Orange markers for works in progress;
    Green markers for finished works.
General graphs
In this section, it is possible to see two main types of graphs. The first one represents, on the X axis the "damage index", that is to say, for each Comune, the sum of the number of works that have never started and the number of works in progress, divided by the number of schools. Thus, for each school there is a number of incomplete works: an index of incompleteness. On the Y axis, instead, there is the school score, for each year respectively.
The other type of graph represents the trend of the total number of works related to damage.
Works chart
For each clicked area, it is possible to find a count of work progress.
